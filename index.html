About Me

I'm a software engineer on the Voice AI Infrastructure team at Cresta Intelligence Inc. (2024–present), where I build the backend systems that connect our conversational AI to the contact-center platforms enterprises actually use. My work has spanned integrations with major CCaaS platforms like Gladly and Avaya AES — deployed at enterprise customers across retail, telecom, and home security — and I'm currently building the backend for an AI receptionist product (agent builder, billing, and insights modules).

Before Cresta, I worked as a software engineer at Inspur USA Inc. (2022–2024) on database system development, and as a research scientist at the JD.com Silicon Valley R&D Center (2021–2022), where I focused on privacy-preserving computation.

I hold an M.S. in Computer Science from Georgia Tech (2021), where I worked with Prof. Alexandra Boldyreva on anonymous review systems and Prof. Vladimir Kolesnikov on MPC protocols. Previously, I received my B.S. in Computer Science from Shanghai Jiao Tong University (2017), where I was a student of the ACM Honored Program at Zhiyuan College and did research with Prof. Thomas Ristenpart as an intern at Cornell Tech.
